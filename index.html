Apostolic Bible Studies welcome! We're happy you're here, discovering the vibrant principles of Apostolic Pentecostal faith. As you navigate our studies, we invite you to live the transforming principles of the Bible and the unique characteristics of Apostolic Pentecostalism, grounded in the transforming power of Jesus Christ. Apostolic Pentecostalism is one of the currents within Christianity that professes the faith and traditions of the early church as recorded in the New Testament. The movement of Apostolic Pentecostalism heavily focuses on the full gospelâ€”namely, on repentance, baptism by the name of Jesus, and receiving the infilling of the Holy Spirit. It is a movement founded upon the belief in the unity of God, believing Jesus Christ to be the one true God, made known in the flesh. It is not in accordance with traditional Trinitarian doctrine, practicing the biblical truth that God is one, and that Jesus is the manifestation of the one God. Pentecostalism, as one of the significant aspects of the Apostolic faith, focuses on the importance of the gifts of the Spirit and the manifestation of the Holy Spirit in the believer's life. Among these gifts is the gift of speaking in tongues, which is the sign of being filled with the Holy Spirit. At the heart of Apostolic Pentecostalism is a personal and intimate relationship with Jesus Christ. We believe that every believer is called to be holy and sanctified, following in Christ's example and spreading His love and truth to other people. Our guide is the Bible, and its principles are the supports upon which we build our lives. Our heart's desire is to give you the wisdom and knowledge to live your faith authentically and boldly, as the first Apostles. Whether you are beginning your walk or have been walking in faith for decades, we feel that God's Word has something dynamic to say to you today. Thank you for stopping by Apostolic Bible Studies. May you be blessed as you study the teachings of Scripture and come to experience the life-altering power of the gospel of Jesus Christ.
