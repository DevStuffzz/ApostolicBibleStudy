Who is Jesus?


In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. (John 1:1, KJV)
And the Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us, (and we beheld his glory, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father,) full of grace and truth.(John 1:14, KJV)

Simply put, Jesus is this flesh that is talked about in John chapter one. He is God incarnate, and was truly man and truly God. Jesus is not a separate person from the Father; He is the manifestation of the one true God, who revealed Himself in the flesh. In John 14:9, Jesus declares, "He that hath seen me hath seen the Father." This speaks to the understanding that Jesus, in His entirety, is the Father made visible. Jesus is not one of three co-equal persons but the full and complete expression of God’s nature in one person.

As the One God, Jesus fully embodies the divine nature and purpose of God’s plan for salvation. Through His death, burial, and resurrection, He provided the means for humanity to be reconciled with God. In 1 Timothy 3:16, it says, "And without controversy great is the mystery of godliness: God was manifest in the flesh." This is a clear declaration that the eternal God, the Creator, was revealed in the person of Jesus Christ. There is no distinction between the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit as separate persons in the Godhead; rather, these are different roles or manifestations of the one God. Jesus, as the Son, was God Himself coming to earth to fulfill the work of salvation.

In John 1:14, it states, "And the Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us, (and we beheld his glory, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father,) full of grace and truth." This emphasizes that Jesus is the Word, the eternal Logos, made flesh. He is the full revelation of God’s will to humanity. Jesus did not merely represent God; He was God in flesh.

Thus, we baptize in the name of Jesus because He is the revealed name of God (Acts 2:38). In Matthew 28:19, when Jesus commanded His disciples to baptize in the name of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, He was speaking of the name of Jesus, the only name by which we are saved. Jesus, being the name of the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost, signifies the fullness of the Godhead in one name.

The fullness of the Godhead dwells in Jesus Christ (Colossians 2:9), and He is the only mediator between God and man. Jesus is not a lesser being but the manifestation of the eternal God Himself. In Him, we find all the fullness of deity, and in His name, we have salvation.

In John 10:30, Jesus states, "I and my Father are one." This oneness speaks to the unity of God in Jesus Christ. It is not a division of persons but the unity of the one God, manifest in flesh to redeem mankind. This is the simple truth that is revealed all throught scripture: there is one God, and that one God is revealed fully in Jesus Christ.

Thus, when we worship Jesus, we worship the one true God, who is both our Savior and our Creator. The plan of salvation in Jesus is the culmination of God's eternal purpose, revealed to us in the person of Christ. Through Him, we find life, reconciliation, and eternal hope.

However since every single person has sinned (Romans 3:23), God had to robe himself in flesh (1 Timothy 3:16) and come down to earth, in order to be his own perfect vessel. This distinction between the Father and the Son simple refers to the difference between two manifestations of God, both as God and as man. There is but one God. (Deuteronomy 6:4)

Jesus as a human

Jesus was in every way human like us, however he lived without sin. This flesh remained obidient unto death, so God highly exhaulted him, and gave him the Name above all names (Philippians 2:9). Jesus was born in the town of Bethleham, and was the son of Mary and Joseph. His parents were engaged at the time, and an angel came to Mary telling her that she would give birth to Jesus, the Son of God, despite her being still a virgin. (Luke 1:26-35)

Coming of Jesus prophecied in the Old Testament

It should come as no surprise that the life of Jesus was prophesied in the Old Testament. Here are some of the key prophecies He fulfilled:

  • Be a descendant of King David – Psalm 89:3, Jeremiah 23:5
  • Be born of a virgin – Isaiah 7:14
  • Be born in Bethlehem – Micah 5:2
  • Be a descendant of Abraham – Genesis 22:18, Genesis 12:3
  • Be a descendant of Isaac – Genesis 21:12
  • Be a descendant of Jacob – Numbers 24:17
  • Be a descendant of Judah – Genesis 49:10
  • Be called Immanuel ("God with us") – Isaiah 7:14
  • Have a messenger prepare His way (John the Baptist) – Isaiah 40:3-5, Malachi 3:1
  • Perform miracles – Isaiah 35:5-6
  • Be rejected by His own people – Psalm 118:22, Isaiah 53:3
  • Be betrayed by a close friend – Psalm 41:9, Zechariah 11:12-13
  • Be silent before His accusers – Isaiah 53:7
  • Be crucified with criminals – Isaiah 53:12
  • Have His hands and feet pierced – Psalm 22:16, Zechariah 12:10
  • Have His garments divided and lots cast for them – Psalm 22:18
  • Die as a sacrifice for sin – Isaiah 53:5-12
  • Rise from the dead – Psalm 16:10, Hosea 6:2
